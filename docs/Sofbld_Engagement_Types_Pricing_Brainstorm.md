# Sofbld Engagement Types & Pricing Brainstorm

## Context

Sofbld will not always be pursuing only large "home run" software builds.

There is also a strong opportunity to take on:

- focused optimization work
- UX and conversion improvements
- performance and workflow improvements
- AI assistant and chatbot implementations
- internal knowledge assistants
- lead qualification automations
- smaller AI enablement projects

The pricing and positioning should make room for both:

- larger custom product builds
- smaller, outcome-driven engagements

The challenge is to expand the offer without making the brand feel cheap, overly packaged, or less premium.

---

## Core Recommendation

Instead of framing pricing only around project size, Sofbld can frame pricing around **engagement type**.

That creates space for:

- big custom builds
- smaller optimization projects
- practical AI implementations
- ongoing support relationships

This keeps the brand flexible while still feeling deliberate and premium.

---

## Suggested Engagement Types

### 1. Strategy Sprint

For:

- scoping
- architecture
- roadmap planning
- idea validation
- technical direction

Positioning:

This is the starting point for clients who need clarity before committing to a larger build.

### 2. Optimization Sprint

For:

- conversion improvements
- UX cleanup
- workflow simplification
- feature refinement
- performance improvements
- targeted operational fixes

Positioning:

This is the right offer for companies that do not need a full rebuild, but do need meaningful gains in product quality or business performance.

### 3. AI Enablement Sprint

For:

- internal knowledge assistants
- customer support automation
- lead qualification bots
- AI copilots embedded in workflows
- prompt and knowledge-base setup
- lightweight AI tools tied to real operations

Positioning:

This creates a place for practical AI work without forcing it into a full-scale custom platform engagement.

Note:

Public-facing language should avoid making these sound too small or gimmicky. "AI assistant," "support automation," and "knowledge bot" feel stronger than "simple chatbot."

### 4. Product Builds

For:

- MVPs
- customer-facing products
- internal platforms
- advanced custom software
- more complex systems with integrations and domain-specific requirements

Positioning:

This remains the core premium offer and includes both focused first versions and larger growth-stage builds.

### 5. Ongoing Engineering Support

For:

- iterative product improvements
- maintenance
- testing and refinement
- feature additions
- AI enhancements
- continuous optimization

Positioning:

This supports clients who already have a system in place and want a reliable partner for ongoing progress.

---

## Website Recommendation

It likely makes sense to create a separate **Engagement Types & Pricing** page.

### Why this helps

The homepage pricing section can stay short, clean, and high-trust.

The deeper page can do the heavier work of:

- explaining how pricing works
- helping visitors self-qualify
- showing the difference between engagement types
- making room for both large builds and focused engagements
- clarifying that Sofbld is flexible without becoming vague

### Suggested structure for that page

- overview of how Sofbld approaches pricing
- engagement types
- who each engagement is for
- starting ranges or typical ranges
- what is included
- example engagements
- factors that affect pricing
- FAQ
- call to action

---

## Positioning Guardrails

To keep the brand premium:

- do not present smaller engagements as bargain packages
- do not overuse "small project" language
- focus on business outcomes, not cheap task execution
- use "starting at" or "typical range" rather than rigid fixed pricing
- maintain a meaningful minimum engagement floor

The message should be:

Sofbld can handle both ambitious builds and focused high-impact engagements, but everything is approached with the same strategic and engineering rigor.

---

## Messaging Direction

A strong top-level framing could be:

> Sofbld helps clients scope, improve, automate, build, and scale software systems.

This works well because it naturally includes:

- strategy work
- optimization work
- AI enablement
- full builds
- ongoing support

It broadens the offer without diluting the brand.

---

## Key Takeaway

The goal is not to move away from premium custom software.

The goal is to show that Sofbld can enter the relationship at different points:

- when a client needs clarity
- when a client needs improvement
- when a client needs automation
- when a client needs a full custom product
- when a client needs an ongoing engineering partner

That makes the pricing model feel more realistic, more flexible, and more aligned with the kinds of work Sofbld may actually win.
